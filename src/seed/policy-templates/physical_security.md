## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes requirements for protecting {{organisation_name}}'s physical premises, equipment, and information assets from physical threats, unauthorised access, and environmental hazards.

## 2. Scope

{{scope}}

## 3. Physical Security Perimeters

Security perimeters shall be defined for all areas containing sensitive information or critical systems. Perimeter security controls shall include:
- Physically sound walls, floors, and ceilings for secure areas
- Controlled entry points with appropriate access control mechanisms
- Clear definition and demarcation of security boundaries
- Regular inspection of physical security perimeters

## 4. Access Control

### 4.1 General Areas
- Visitor access must be pre-authorised and logged
- Visitors must be escorted by an authorised employee at all times in secure areas
- Visitor badges must be visually distinguishable from employee badges

### 4.2 Secure Areas
- Access to secure areas (server rooms, data centres, secure storage) requires explicit authorisation
- Access shall be controlled by electronic access control with audit logging
- Access rights shall be reviewed regularly and revoked promptly when no longer needed
- Tailgating and piggybacking are prohibited

### 4.3 Physical Security Monitoring
Premises shall be monitored using CCTV or equivalent monitoring systems. Monitoring data shall be retained for a minimum of 90 days. Intrusion detection systems shall be deployed in secure areas.

## 5. Clear Desk and Clear Screen

- Sensitive documents must not be left unattended on desks
- Computer screens must be locked when left unattended (automatic lock after 5 minutes of inactivity)
- Sensitive documents must be stored in locked cabinets or drawers when not in use
- Printers and copiers must be cleared of sensitive documents immediately after use
- Whiteboards containing sensitive information must be erased after meetings

## 6. Equipment Protection

- Equipment must be sited to minimise exposure to environmental and physical threats
- Laptops and portable equipment must not be left unattended in public places
- Equipment taken off-site requires prior authorisation
- Data on portable equipment must be encrypted
- Cables, conduits, and network connections shall be protected from interference and damage

## 7. Supporting Utilities

- Critical systems must be protected by UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply)
- Regular testing of UPS and generator systems shall be conducted
- HVAC systems supporting IT equipment must be maintained and monitored
- Utility failures must be reported immediately

## 8. Equipment Maintenance and Disposal

- Equipment must be maintained in accordance with manufacturer specifications
- Only authorised personnel may perform maintenance on IT equipment
- Maintenance activities on sensitive equipment must be supervised
- Equipment containing data must be securely wiped or physically destroyed before disposal
- Disposal or re-use shall be documented and certified for sensitive equipment

## 9. Environmental Threats

Physical security shall address protection against fire, flood, earthquake, power failure, and other environmental threats through:
- Fire detection and suppression systems
- Water detection systems in server rooms
- Environmental monitoring (temperature, humidity)
- Regular testing of physical security measures
